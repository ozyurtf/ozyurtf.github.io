Since I read "Incognito" (by David Eagleman) in 2018, reading and learning about neuroscience and (trying to) understand how our brains work has been one of my favorite hobbies. Until a couple of months ago, I had been reading mostly non-technical books, but I decided to dive into more detail and learn more about different brain regions (e.g., hippocampus, thalamus, parietal cortex, entorhinal cortex, perirhinal cortex, occipital cortex, etc.), their impacts on our daily lives, the moments when these regions are activated, how they communicate with each other, and so on. While reading these books, I stop and write down my understanding of the sections from my point of view, and you can see the notes I took below.
